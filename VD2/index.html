The three visualization are linked together based on age and gender, which means whenever you hover on any bar on each of visualizations the same data will be highlighted on other visualizations too. Implementing the highlight on the third visualization was a bit tricky. The idea is the sankey links are drawn as paths in the svg element, which is calculated and generated by D3. Thus, considering that the data variables on the other two visualizations is different than the ones on the sankey, we only want to highlight a protion of those links, which is actually impossible. Therefore, we have to mimic highlighting somehow. What we did was to calculate the numbers as we did for sankey visualization, but this time with age included. Then draw another sankey on top of the one we have but this time draw links with a width of "age based data / actual data". This strategy is not perfect but at least shows the proportion of data related to each bar in the sankey. However, the opposite direction is not something interesting to implement, so the hovering action on the sankey will have no effect.
